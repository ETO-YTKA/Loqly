## Design Context

### Users
Loqly is for close friends using chat in an informal, everyday context. The primary job is fast casual conversation: sending quick messages, checking in, reacting in the moment, and keeping lightweight social contact alive without the formality of team chat or the noise of large community platforms.

### Brand Personality
Loqly should feel fun, cozy, and young. The interface should feel warm and approachable rather than polished-corporate, loud, or hyper-technical. The emotional goal is to make messaging feel welcoming, light, and socially intimate, with enough playfulness to feel alive without becoming chaotic.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is cozy playful, with support for both light and dark mode. Pink should be treated as a core brand color and used with restraint for emotional warmth and recognizable personality rather than as a constant high-saturation accent. The interface should avoid generic messenger neutrality and instead feel soft, expressive, and friendly, with rounded forms, warm-tinted neutrals, and small moments of charm that reinforce conversation between close friends.

### Design Principles
1. Design for intimacy over utility theater: every screen should feel made for friendship, not enterprise communication.
2. Keep chat fast and casual: interactions should feel immediate, lightweight, and easy to read at a glance.
3. Use warmth deliberately: pink and warm-tinted surfaces should create comfort without overwhelming the interface.
4. Stay playful without becoming noisy: motion, color, and details should add charm while preserving clarity.
5. Support both day and night use: light and dark themes should each feel intentional, not like one is just an inverted fallback.
